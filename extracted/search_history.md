Short answer: No-ibuprofen (Advil) does not meaningfully reduce the effectiveness of
sildenafil (Viagra) or tadalafil (Cialis).
Pharmacologic rationale
• Viagra (sildenafil) and Cialis (tadalafil) are PDE-5 inhibitors. They enhance nitric
oxide-mediated vasodilation in the corpus cavernosum.
• Ibuprofen is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID). It inhibits cyclooxygenase
(COX-1/COX-2) and reduces prostaglandin synthesis.
• These drugs act through different biochemical pathways, and there is no clinically
significant interaction known between them.
What to consider
• Blood pressure: PDE-5 inhibitors lower blood pressure modestly. Ibuprofen does not
typically lower blood pressure; if anything, chronic NSAID use can slightly raise it.
Taking them together does not usually cause hypotension.
• Headache management: PDE-5 inhibitors commonly cause headache due to
vasodilation. Ibuprofen is often taken to treat that headache and is generally acceptable
for that purpose.
• Gastrointestinal risk: If you are prone to reflux or GI irritation, both alcohol and
NSAIDs can aggravate symptoms, but this does not impair erectile response.
• Cardiovascular status: The more important safety consideration with PDE-5 inhibitors
is concurrent use of nitrates or certain alpha-blockers-not NSAIDs.
Bottom line
Taking Advil at the same time as Viagra or Cialis should not diminish their effectiveness and
is generally considered safe for most otherwise healthy men.
If you have cardiovascular disease, kidney disease, are on blood pressure medications, or take
nitrates, the analysis changes and you should consult your physician.
